Phishing Alert

Someone just tried to scam you!

The link you just clicked is part of a scam to get your personal information.

This website was hacked. The hackers created deceptive pages designed to look like real sites that you may use (such as Dropbox, Google, or Adobe).

The goal was for you to enter your login credentials, giving the hackers access to your account on those sites. This is a scam commonly called "phishing".

The fake pages have been removed and replaced with this warning message.

Learn how to avoid phishing

What Is Phishing?

Phishing is the attempt to acquire sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details (and sometimes, indirectly, money), often for malicious reasons, by masquerading as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication.

This case was what is known as a "Website Forgery" where the attackers used common logos and colors to pretend like they were a legitimate site.

What Should I Do Now?

If you didn't fill out one of the scammer's forms, you should be OK.

However, it's always a good idea to change your passwords regularly. You should also set up two factor authentication if possible. This helps you keep safe even if the actual text of your password is compromised.

Running regular system scans and an anti-virus software is also a good idea.

Why didn't you just remove the page?

Scams like this are caught and removed all over the net. But there's nothing for the victim to learn from just clicking a malicious link and getting a "not found" or "forbidden" message.

In fact, taking it down might be worse if the victim replies to the email to ask why the link doesn't work, then starts interacting with the hackers directly.

Hopefully this warning prevents future scams, and not just this one.
